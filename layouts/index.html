We are a research group at {{ site.Data.site.institution }} in Edinburgh, based at the National Robotarium. We build AI for medical imaging and robotics that works across hospitals, devices and datasets: models that excel in one setting often fail at the next scanner, hospital or robot, and we develop learning methods that carry capability across those differences — onto the clinical and robotic terminals where AI is actually used.

Recent work includes two Nature Communications studies measuring bone marrow adiposity across roughly 47,000 UK Biobank participants, and SeLoRA, an open-source method for adapting large image-synthesis models to specialised medical tasks.

The group is led by {{ site.Data.site.principal_investigator }}, {{ site.Data.site.pi_position }}. Contact: {{ site.Data.site.email }}.

Research framework

  • Cloud — population and foundation models. Hospitals and biobanks hold imaging data at population scale; we build large-scale measurement, synthesis and representation-learning methods there, for example the UK Biobank bone marrow imaging pipeline behind our Nature Communications studies.
  • Edge — heterogeneous collaboration. Hospitals and partners cannot always pool data or run identical hardware; federated learning and knowledge transfer let models learn across institutions, architectures and devices while source data stays under local governance.
  • Terminal — intelligence at the point of use. Scanners, surgical systems and robots run on fixed hardware with strict time budgets; efficient adaptation and compression put large-model capability onto the clinical and robotic terminals where it is used.
