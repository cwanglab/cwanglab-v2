Bone marrow adipose tissue is a distinct fat depot linked to bone health, metabolic disease and treatment response, but it has been difficult to measure at population scale. This collaboration developed a deep-learning pipeline that quantifies bone marrow fat fraction in four skeletal regions — spine, femoral head, total hip and femoral diaphysis — from UK Biobank Dixon MRI, and validated it for large-scale use.

The imaging phenotypes produced by the pipeline underpinned two 2025 studies in *Nature Communications*. The first applied genome-wide association meta-analyses to measurements from approximately 47,000 participants, establishing the heritability of marrow adiposity and identifying genetic loci associated with it across ancestry groups. The second examined clinical implications, using phenome-wide association and Mendelian randomisation to map which diseases are associated with — and potentially influenced by — marrow adiposity, including evidence relating it to osteoporosis and fracture risk.

The measurement method itself was published in *Computational and Structural Biotechnology Journal* and is open access, as are both *Nature Communications* papers. Access to the underlying UK Biobank data is governed by UK Biobank rather than distributed by this website; derived measurements have been returned to UK Biobank for use by approved researchers.
