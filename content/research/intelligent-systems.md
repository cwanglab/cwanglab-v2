Beyond medical imaging, we study heterogeneous agents and robotic systems, including perception, planning and coordination across devices with different capabilities. This work connects machine learning to the sensing, communication and control constraints of physical systems.

## Research questions

- How can a robot infer both its own changing morphology and external contact?
- How should perception, decision and control be divided across heterogeneous sensors and processors?
- How can multiple agents with different capabilities coordinate under real physical constraints?

## Methods

This direction combines multimodal sensing, learned inverse models, embodied perception, path planning and distributed decision-making. The objective is to close the sensing-perception-action loop rather than optimise an isolated classifier.
