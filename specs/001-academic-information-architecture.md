# Spec 001: Academic information architecture

## Problem

The current site presents unverified live metrics, promotional framing, duplicated pages and anonymous member placeholders. It does not read as a maintained academic record.

## Required behavior

- The site identifies the group, institution, research questions and contact route above the fold.
- Top-level navigation is Research, People, Publications, Projects & Code, News and Join. The brand links home.
- Home contains research themes, selected outputs and recent updates. It contains no citation counter, h-index, funding total, team count or unsupported compute ratio.
- People contains only named people whose public display is supported. Alumni are a People category, not a separate top-level page.
- Join states funding status conservatively and routes applicants to the official university process or a targeted email.
- Every externally verifiable output links to DOI, arXiv, code, data or a project page when available.

## Acceptance criteria

- No public `#` links, placeholder names, placeholder avatars, `Coming Soon`, or unqualified recruiting claims.
- No About or Alumni item in the top navigation.
- A visitor can reach any core section in one click from any page.
- Page titles, descriptions and heading hierarchy are valid and unique.
